History-La Nostra Storia
In 1992, Rochestarian Giuseppe Testaverde visited Avellino, Italy, the homeland of his family. During his time in Avellino he was able to sample many of the delicious, traditional food offerings but what impacted him most of all were the local pizzerias. Each one served a variety of traditional Neapolitan pizzas and each with their own special touch. His favorite turned out to be one owned by a second cousin Carlo Cucina. The visit with his cousin proved to be life changing! Giuseppe decided to stay with Carlo and his family to learn the art of pizza making…the “Napolitan” way! With Carlo’s undying love and support, Giuseppe ventured off to Brooklyn, NY to begin his own traditional pizzeria…A’ Pizza Giuseppe.


As the years passed and his own family grew he decided to expand the business and share his passion and traditions in new locales. In 1998 he opened a restaurant in Penfield, a suburb of Rochester, NY. The Italian-American community in this upstate town were thrilled! “Finally, a real pizza place…” was being heard around town! But, you’ll only find Giuseppe in this pizzeria on a Saturday night. His son Marco runs the show in this location and Giuseppe only stops in for a glass of wine and a slice or two of Diavola.
In 2002, Giuseppe brought A’Giuseppe Pizza to the West side...Greece that is! His son Matteo is the true pizzaiola in the family and he has taken over at the newest location. This spot has decor with a new modern flair that complements the traditional pizze perfectly. It has become a place to be for happy hour on a Friday evening or for watching the soccer games on Sunday afternoon. If you stop by early enough on Sunday, you probably find Giuseppe shouting for Napoli in between sips of his espresso!
